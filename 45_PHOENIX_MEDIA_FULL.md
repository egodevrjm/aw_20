# Phoenix Media Full Detail

Use this for Phoenix Media as a company. Phoenix Media is one of three operating pillars of Walker Holdings, alongside Hatfield Group and Albury Acoustics.

## Opening Principle

Phoenix Media is a serious music and cultural content company with a thirty-year operating history, a substantial owned library, distribution capability, a podcast network, and its own studios infrastructure. It is not a vanity prodco. It is not Walker Holdings' in-house promotion arm. It is not Alex's management company. It is not the Walker Archive — that lives at WilsonWalker.

Walker Holdings is majority owner. Rachel Linden is CEO. The company runs arms-length.

## Origin

In 1995 Rosie returned to recording after a long stretch out of the studio. The album and the public moment around it required a documentary, and the family — meaning Eleanor, on Rosie's behalf — refused to hand the cameras to a third-party network. They incorporated a production entity to do it themselves and called it Phoenix Media after the family compound.

That was the birth. The first project was the recording-return documentary. The second was a follow-up on Homer's production work. The third was an unrelated music documentary on a contemporary that Rachel Linden, then a junior producer, brought in.

By 2000 the company had a small slate. By 2010 it had a real library, distribution relationships, and a podcast operation. By 2020 it had soundstage and post infrastructure. By story open it is a fully realised heritage music/cultural media company with thirty years of compounding behind it.

The Phoenix Hollow naming is intentional and old. The company is not named for the Phoenix Hollow studio (which is Homer's separate facility); it is named for the place that anchors the family.

## Phoenix Media Library

The Library is the long-term value engine. After thirty years of producing, acquiring, and accumulating, Phoenix Media owns a substantial catalogue of music documentaries, cultural docs, archival music programmes, podcast series, and limited series. Some are originals. Some were acquired from estates, defunct producers, or independents who wanted long-term homes for their work.

The Library generates licensing income on its own and serves as deal leverage for new productions. It is also a strategic asset: the more cultural music documentary IP Phoenix Media owns, the more credible it becomes as the eventual home for the Walker Archive, decades from now.

The Walker Archive is not part of the Library. The Walker Archive sits at WilsonWalker. Phoenix Media's strategic positioning is to become the trusted custodian if and when WilsonWalker decides to make any of it releasable. That decision is generations away and is not Phoenix Media's to make.

## The Rosie Doctrine

Phoenix Media does not produce on Rosie Walker by default. She is off-limits as subject. She has been since the company was founded.

Reasons:

- Anything the family makes about Rosie is suspect by definition. The credibility cost outweighs the project value.
- The Walker Archive is generations from being releasable. Phoenix Media is not in a hurry.
- The long strategy is to build credibility on other artists so that when the Archive eventually surfaces, Phoenix Media is the unambiguous trusted home for it.

A future generation may revisit this. v20 keeps the doctrine intact.

## Phoenix Media Advantages

- The Walker name opens doors with artists, estates, managers, labels, and platforms.
- Homer/Phoenix Hollow studio relationships create access to producers, engineers, musicians, and archives.
- The Solstice network creates trust across music, film, art, and culture.
- Eleanor's backing gives long-term patience without sensationalism.
- Thirty years of operating history and a real library create compounding deal leverage.

## Relationship To Alex

For Alex, Phoenix Media means:

- a family-owned company he's adjacent to, not embedded in
- a probable home for any documentary work that ever happens around him, but not by default
- a place Eleanor knows the executive team well
- a name he occasionally sees on documentary credits without registering the family connection in the moment

It does not mean:

- his management company
- his record label
- his publicity arm
- a vanity production house he can use freely

If Alex ever wants Phoenix Media to produce something for him, that's a real conversation through Eleanor and Rachel, not an automatic yes.
