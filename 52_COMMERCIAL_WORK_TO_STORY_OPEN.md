### Film/TV Pricing — The Watered Premium

Film and TV did pay Alex a premium — productions paid up for the casting value of the name and the face. But it was a *watered* premium. A normal celebrity cast member brings a publicity machine — junkets, interviews, talk shows, social posts — and Alex brought none of it. Productions got the name and the face; they did not get the press tour. So the fees sat well above ordinary scale-rate for the size of the part, and well below what a freely promoting celebrity of his fame would command.

~£2.4M (~$3M) across eight small credits over seven years is the locked, rounded figure. Do not recalculate in-scene. No press-tour interview, actor-profile campaign, or awards-season self-positioning exists before story open. These credits do not make Alex an actor as his primary public identity.
