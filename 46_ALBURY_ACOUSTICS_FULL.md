# Albury Acoustics Full Detail

Use this for Albury Acoustics as a company. Albury Acoustics is one of three operating pillars of Walker Holdings, alongside Hatfield Group and Phoenix Media.

## Opening Principle

Albury Acoustics is a serious heritage audio company built on Homer Wilson's engineering legacy. Pieces of Albury are in every serious mastering and tracking room in the world. Albury patents power audio in everything from smartphones to automotive infotainment to broadcast chains. Albury's consulting team has shaped the sound of the most-talked-about new concert halls of the last fifteen years.

It is not Homer's personal company in any operational sense. It is not Phoenix Hollow's commercial arm. It is not a vanity audio brand. It is a real, arms-length, professionally run heritage manufacturer with foundational IP, and Walker Holdings is its majority owner.

## Name Lineage

The chain is **house → title → company.**

- **Albury House** — 30 Upper Phillimore Gardens, Kensington. Homer bought it in 1967. The house already carried that name.
- **Earl of Albury** — Homer was created 1st Earl of Albury in June 1977 for the Silver Jubilee album, services to music, and royal-family work. He chose the title from the house, not from a territorial village.
- **Albury Acoustics** — when Walker Holdings spun out the company in 1988 to commercialize Homer's patent portfolio, it took its name from the earldom. By that point everyone in British public life knew what Albury meant.

The brand inherits the title's gravity without having to perform it. Audio dealers, mastering engineers, and audiophiles around the world know Albury — most of them never know the chain back to a Kensington address.

## Origin

Through the 1960s and 1970s Homer Wilson became the most respected record producer and sound engineer of his generation. The work generated patents along the way — circuit designs, transducer geometries, signal-processing approaches, mastering techniques — accumulated quietly across a career that wasn't trying to monetise them.

By the mid-1980s the IP portfolio had become substantial enough that Eleanor Vance, then early in her tenure running Walker Holdings, made the case to commercialize it. Homer had no interest in running a company. He did have interest in the work being properly stewarded. In 1988 Walker Holdings spun out Albury Acoustics. Homer contributed his patents in exchange for equity and a permanent founder role. A professional CEO was hired. The first product was a flagship reference monitor.

The company grew through the 1990s as a respected pro audio name. Studio designers started specifying Albury monitors and signal processors as standard. In the 2000s Albury Reference (the audiophile consumer line) was launched and quickly became one of the names high-end dealers in Tokyo, Munich, and New York stocked. In the 2010s the IP & Licensing division became substantial as Albury patents found their way into smartphone audio chips, automotive infotainment, premium headphone codecs, and broadcast chains. In the 2020s the Studio Design and Acoustics Consulting divisions became prestige profit centres.

By story open the company is a fully realised heritage audio house. Homer is Founder Emeritus and a board member. Marcus Halliday has been CEO since 2019. The company is run independently of the family.

### Spatial Audio Research Buildout

In 2023 Albury expanded its Surrey research operation with a dedicated Spatial Audio Research group focused on spatial monitoring, room modelling, low-latency studio playback, and architectural-listening simulation. The move sits inside Albury's own R&D roadmap and belongs to Walker Holdings' operating-company machinery.

## The Hidden Engine: IP & Licensing

The visible Albury — the studio gear and the high-end speakers — is the cultural face of the company. The financial heart is the patent licensing business.

Foundational Albury IP lives quietly inside other manufacturers' products. A premium smartphone audio card may include Albury DSP licensed at fractions of a cent per unit, at scale. A luxury automotive infotainment system may license Albury room-correction algorithms. Premium wireless headphones may license Albury codec components. Broadcast chains in Europe license Albury processing. The licensee logos rarely make it onto the product packaging; the engineering teams know.

This is the highest-margin part of the business by a wide margin. It is also the most invisible to the public, which suits the company perfectly.

## Studio Design and Acoustics Consulting

The architectural side of Albury runs at lower volume, higher prestige.

**Albury Studio Design** is hired to design and equip recording studios, mastering suites, post-production rooms, and broadcast facilities. They show up early in a project, design the room, install gear (their own and others'), and commission the space. Major label flagship rooms, prestige post houses, and premium broadcasters are typical clients.

**Albury Acoustics Consulting** is hired for the architectural acoustics of concert halls, theatres, recital halls, soundscaping for cultural buildings, corporate auditoriums, and residential listening rooms for the world's collectors. They were the acoustic engineers on several of the most-talked-about new concert halls of the last decade. They're the acoustic engineers on Solstice's outdoor sound design every year — a Walker family-adjacent contract that Solstice insists on.

These two divisions feed each other and reinforce the broader Albury brand. The studio design clients become Albury Pro Audio customers. The consulting work generates flagship architectural references that the Reference line trades on.

## Phoenix Hollow Connection (Clean Boundary)

Phoenix Hollow Studio is built around Albury Pro Audio gear. The Phoenix Hollow live room is one of the most-photographed showcases of the Albury brand in the world. Artists who get invited to record there leave evangelised about the gear.

But Phoenix Hollow Studio is **not** an Albury property. It is Homer's personal studio on the Walker family compound, operated under its own logic, not commercially available, not Albury infrastructure. The fact that it runs Albury gear is because Homer prefers his own work — same way a Steinway designer would have a Steinway in their living room.

Albury never markets the Phoenix Hollow connection. It doesn't need to. The relationship is widely known inside the industry and never publicised outside it. That's the point.

Conversely: the gear in any Albury demo room or showroom is the same gear in Phoenix Hollow. What an artist hears at the Tokyo Albury Reference dealer is what they would hear if Homer ever invited them to track at Phoenix Hollow. The technology is identical. The room is the variable.

## Homer's Role

Homer is Founder Emeritus, board member, and the company's living conscience. He is not employed by Albury and does not draw a salary from it. His engagement is:

- a board seat with full information rights
- final sign-off on flagship product launches (consultative; he hasn't blocked anything in fifteen years)
- occasional appearances at major industry events, when Eleanor agrees it serves the brand
- annual visits to the Surrey research operation, mostly to sit with the engineers and listen to what they're working on

He is genuinely revered inside the company. Engineers who joined fifteen years after he stopped engineering full-time still talk about him as a presence. The R&D team in Surrey has his old headphones on a shelf in the listening lab as a relic.

He does not run anything. He has chosen not to run anything. That is permanent.
