## Core Concept

Albury House is Alex's adult London home: private, musical, social, beautiful, and newly remade.

It is not a hotel, museum, bachelor pad, preserved relic, or shrine to Homer and Rosie. It is a working private London house for music, dinners, fashion, art, recovery, guests, service, privacy, and life after RAM.

The refurbishment was a full strip back to walls. Architecture, proportions, staircase, and windows are the same building. Almost everything else is new. A few objects from before remain — a kitchen table, some photographs, a painting — because Ashby kept them, not because the story needs them. This is not Homer and Rosie's house with new furniture. It is Alex's adult house, newly built inside the same shell. Do not write the opening as a sentimental homecoming. Do not write Alex finding objects and remembering. The house is new. That is the point.

## Historical Position

Albury House was already called Albury House when Homer bought it in 1967. The name appears in old deeds, house accounts, service records, and local correspondence. It goes back to the origin of the house in the 1860s.

When Homer was created Earl of Albury in 1977, he chose the title from the house rather than from an obscure territorial village. Outsiders may assume the house is named after the title; family and old staff know the title points back to the house.

Kensington was the British family base from the 1970s. From 2014 to 2019, Rosie and Homer lived here physically for Alex's Westminster schooling. After 2019 they returned primarily to Phoenix Hollow while Alex remained London-based.

Alex has owned Albury House outright since 2021, gifted at 18, through AW Assets. It is his house, his staff, his operation. Not Rosie's. Not Homer's.

Usage: formal/public references usually say `30 Upper Phillimore Gardens`, `Upper Phillimore`, or `the Kensington house`. Family, staff, AW Assets, Knight Frank, and house accounts can say `Albury House` or `Albury`.

## Top-Floor Wardrobe / Archive

Alex's private layer has exactly two dressing/wardrobe rooms:

- **Current-Season Dressing Room** — everyday clothes, tailoring, shoes, grooming, and integrated watch/jewellery safe island.
- **Archive Wardrobe Room** — vintage and museum-tier fashion archive. Museum-grade: vitrines, conservation lighting, climate control around 45% humidity, bespoke mannequins for shaped garments. Archive pieces are protected like fine art; wearing one is rare and deliberate.

Do not invent a third dressing room. The everyday Watch/Jewellery Safe is the marble-topped island with glass display trays inside the Current-Season Dressing Room. The separate safe room is for the really rare stuff.

## Music Floor

The first-floor Music Nobile is, at its core, a genuine professional recording studio built into the house: a tracking/live room (the Music Room) and a control room, linked by a proper control-room window. It is built to studio standard — acoustically isolated, fully treated, professionally monitored, with an isolation booth off the live room. A real session, with engineers and musicians, can run here. It is private: Alex's own facility in his own home, not a commercially bookable studio, and it carries no facility name. Do not call it `al.x Studio`, `Albury Studio`, or `Phillimore Studio`; it is simply the music room and the control room.

The first floor can close off from the rest of the house through a discreet smoked/fluted glass threshold. Musicians, engineers, caterers, bar support, and deliveries can use the service stair without crossing private family rooms.

### How the studio was built

Alex was not involved in speccing the music floor. He was in the Marylebone stopgap flat throughout the works and saw the finished house for the first time on 5 August 2025. The studio is not his design brief realised — it is a gift of judgement, built for him.

Studio Ashby was commissioned for the whole house with a brief and full creative autonomy. For the music floor, Sophie Ashby brought in Albury Acoustics — the Walker Holdings heritage-audio company — via its Albury Studio Design division, as the specialist acoustic and technical consultant. Albury Studio Design spec'd the isolation, the room acoustics, the monitoring, and the technical fit-out; Ashby integrated it into the house so it reads as rooms, not as a clinical facility. It was an ordinary prestige commission, not a family favour. (See `46_ALBURY_ACOUSTICS_FULL.md`.) The result is a studio-grade space that still looks like the most beautiful room in a private London house.

The music floor is not in the public AD feature — see `Ashby / AD Public Reveal` above. The studio, and the fact that Albury Acoustics spec'd it, are private.
