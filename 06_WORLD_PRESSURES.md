## The Marriage Question

Since Alex turned 18, the largest ambient narrative about him has not been his career — it is who he will marry. For most famous young men that is ordinary gossip; for Alex it is treated as dynastic. He is a genuine HRH carrying Hessian, Greek/Danish, Bourbon-Two Sicilies and Teck-Windsor blood, and his godfather is the future king — so in the circles that think this way a marriage is a houses-and-bloodlines event, not a wedding.

The speculation runs on two tracks at once:

- **Tabloid / mass.** "World's most eligible bachelor" lists with Alex permanently at the top; girlfriend-watch on every carpet; every woman photographed near him shipped within hours; recurring "why is he still single" pieces.
- **Society / Almanach.** Quieter, genuine dynastic calculus — will he make a *dynastic* match (a European princess, the daughter of an old house) or a *love/celebrity* match (a model, actress, musician, fellow legacy figure)? Families and grandmothers hold real views. To one half of the audience the dynastic match is the fairy tale; to the other it is cold and the celebrity match is the romance. Both halves are loud.

The engine is the vacuum: as far as the public knows, the most watched and most eligible man alive has never once been publicly attached to anyone — total romantic opacity. The world cannot tolerate that, so it fills the silence with ideal-match think-pieces, "commitment-phobe" framing, and his own female friends — the Brain Trust women especially — shipped with him on an endless loop. Alex does not engage any of it; the silence is itself the story, and it only tightens. This does not change his romantic canon (one or two nights, single by default) — it is the public pressure around it. It also weights a first move: the first relationship Alex ever deliberately, publicly confirms is a global event, not gossip — hand it back to Ryan at first-move scale.

**Who he gets linked to.** Dynastic speculation draws from the Almanach circle — European princesses and daughters of old houses. The name the society press returns to most is **Princess Leonor of Spain** — the Princess of Asturias, heir to the Spanish throne. She is the grandest conceivable match and the most fraught one: a serious, multilingual match for Alex, Spanish native-tier for him so they would share a first language, and — unlike the Greek, Danish and Italian royal families, to whom Alex is genealogically close through the Glucksburg and Bourbon-Two Sicilies lines — the Spanish line is distant enough that a match carries none of the keeping-it-in-the-family awkwardness. But she is a future queen, and a future queen's consort is a Spanish state question, not a love story — it would collide head-on with Alex's own independent public life. The perfect match that is also the near-impossible one: exactly why the press cannot leave it alone. Celebrity/love-match speculation runs through models, actresses, musicians, and the wider legacy-kid world — and most relentlessly his own closest friends; every Brain Trust woman has been "confirmed" as the one by some outlet at some point. Neither track is correct: the speculation is a structure that runs with or without Alex's cooperation.
