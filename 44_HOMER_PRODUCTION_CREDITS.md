# Homer Wilson Production And Engineering Credits

Use this file when a scene, press note, chat, or music-world artifact needs Homer's deep professional identity.

This file is the complete Homer credit reference.

This file is not just a Rosie-credit list. Homer has a large non-Rosie production and engineering career.

When asked for "all Homer credits," do not answer with only the Rosie albums. Give the locked non-Rosie credits, the Rosie credits, the Silver Jubilee Album, and the HWAM/rights layer.

## Category Lock

Homer Wilson is a record producer and sound engineer with Abbey Road roots. He is not a country producer, Nashville session patriarch, or casual celebrity producer.

Default names:

| Context | Use |
|---|---|
| Household/private | Homer, Papa |
| Music-world/ordinary prose | Homer Wilson, Homer |
| Formal British context | the Earl of Albury, Lord Albury |
| Avoid | Sir Homer; knighted styling; casual title performance |

Homer lives at Phoenix Hollow at story-open. He is not London-based, Sardinia-based, or actively moving through public industry rooms unless Ryan places him there.

## Career Architecture

Homer's career has four layers:

| Layer | Scope | Use In Scenes |
|---|---|---|
| Abbey Road apprenticeship | 1960s Beatles-era engineering work and studio training | serious music historians, engineers, older rock figures |
| public landmark credits | fully named albums/events such as `Honky Chateau`, `Goodbye Yellow Brick Road`, Rosie's albums, and the Silver Jubilee Album | press, music-world chats, awards/profiles |
| royalty-stream credits | work with Beatles, Pink Floyd, Bowie, Zeppelin, Springsteen, Rolling Stones, Elton, Alicia Keys, and others | business notes, HWAM, archive/reissue discussions |
| Phoenix Hollow era | selective production, remastering, technical IP, Rosie return albums | private studio scenes, WilsonWalker/HWAM context |

Do not flatten this into "Homer produced Rosie's albums." That is only one part of the career.

| Annual revenue | $12M from production royalties |
| Technology licensing | custom audio equipment modification, microphone design, signal-chain optimization, recording techniques (note: foundational audio patents were spun out to Albury Acoustics in 1988 and are no longer held by HWAM) |
| Annual technology revenue | $3M |
| Credit usage licensing | documentaries, music histories, educational material |
| Annual credit/licensing revenue | $750K |
| Studio IP | Phoenix Hollow console specifications, acoustic design, custom equipment, technical documentation |
| Archive | session tapes, notes, technical documentation, photographs, and Homer's own career materials |

This matters because Homer is not merely "Rosie's husband who produced her later records." He has a monetized, administered, historically significant production estate.

## Phoenix Hollow Studio

Phoenix Hollow Studio is Homer's home studio and the sound centre of Rosie's post-soft-stop work.

Use it for:

- Rosie return albums
- private technical work
- selective invited sessions
- Alex listening, practice, and technical education
- music-world intimacy that is private rather than public-facing

Do not use it for:

- Nashville industry defaults
- public release events
- open celebrity drop-ins
- country jam shorthand
- random people wandering into Rosie's or Homer's work

Homer can advise Alex technically. He does not own Alex's music and does not turn every Alex scene into family-legacy instruction.
