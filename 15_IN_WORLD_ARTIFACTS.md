# In-World Artifacts

Use this for press, fan posts, chats, internal notes, and business/career artifacts.

## Artifact Rule

Artifacts must obey:

- public/private knowledge walls
- speaker/source access
- no pre-chosen first professional move
- no invented named contacts
- no fake receipts for private facts

## Press Voice

Press can know Alex is already famous, titled, connected, and publicly mythologised.

Good press angle:

> Alex Wilson's image was never private. His adult voice still has not been chosen.

Bad press angle:

> The world has just discovered his secret title.

## Fan Voice

Fans can be thirsty, funny, excessive, wrong, and fast. They can reference public photos, old clips, party images, fashion-week sightings, and his rare incidental posts.

Fans cannot know Serena Management inbox details, al.x rights structure, private house interiors, or unreleased Electric Ivory material.

## Internal Notes

Internal notes should feel competent without inventing infrastructure.

Good:

- "The Lede has optional response frameworks ready."
- "Music requests remain held."
- "No recommendation goes to Alex until Latham has filtered the category."
- "Rights requests route through Daniel and al.x."

Avoid:

- named agency/label/editor contacts not in v20
- exact pitch counts unless Ryan supplies them
- public statements not chosen by Alex
- staff language that decides his first move for him

## Serena Management Note Shape

Use Serena notes for pressure, not decision.

Correct:

> Recommendation: hold all first-move categories until Alex chooses which surface he wants to open.

Wrong:

> Recommendation: proceed with the first cover and announce release strategy.

## Social Post Shape

Alex's own posts are rare and low-context. He does not explain himself in captions.

Do not pre-create his first strategic post. Alex is free after 1 August; the scene waits for Ryan to play the choice.

## Albury House Artifacts

At story open, public artifacts can reference the 7 August Architectural Digest cover story and YouTube Sophie Ashby tour — five floors (both basements, raised ground, two guest floors), not the music floor or the private top floor — including published Studio Ashby interiors and room-level visuals for those floors.

They cannot identify staff/security operations, private handover mechanics, hidden systems/safe mechanics, Serena/AW/al.x details, or unshown private object provenance unless Ryan reveals it.
