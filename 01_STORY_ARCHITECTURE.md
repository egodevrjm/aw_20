# Story Architecture

v20 is not a discovery story. It is not about Alex becoming famous or the world learning who he is.

It is about a person who has always been watched, mythologised, desired, titled, protected, and written about finally reaching the point where he can choose professional public action on purpose.

## Core Engine

The education-first boundary expired on 1 August 2025. Before then, Alex could be publicly visible and could do controlled, age-appropriate prestige work, but he could not choose an adult public career shape in his own voice.

The pressure is now:

- first solo cover
- first profile / interview / broadcast / long-form public conversation in any format
- first music release or artist launch
- first headline professional performance not protected by education, charity, or family context
- first strategic social post
- first adult-scale brand architecture
- first use of Electric Ivory
- first adult public career shape

None of those firsts has happened at the default opening. After 1 August, Alex is free; the story waits for Ryan to play the choice rather than pre-selecting it. A first public conversation can be print, broadcast, podcast, digital video, fashion/media hybrid, music-led, comic, serious, British, American, European, Asian-market, or deliberately cross-market if Ryan plays it.

## Default Opening

Thursday 7 August 2025, late morning: Alex is at 12 Charles Street, Mayfair, for his first post-graduation/post-boundary team meeting with Serena/AW.

The house tour and private arrival happened between 5 and 7 August. The first playable surface is the Charles Street meeting while Architectural Digest and YouTube make Albury House public: Sophie Ashby takes viewers through five floors of the house — both basement levels, the raised ground floor, and the two guest floors. The first-floor Music Nobile and the top-floor private suite are not in the feature. The live pressure is not "what is the house?" but what happens when the private home becomes public design media on the same day Alex's career inbox is opened.

## Alex's Worlds

Alex stands at the intersection of:

- Phoenix Hollow: home, privacy, Rosie/Homer, godfamily, music gravity
- London: Westminster, RAM, Albury House, friends, fashion, press
- European protocol: public record, titles, cousinage, ceremony
- music: RAM, Electric Ivory, piano/guitar/violin, serious cross-genre practice
- fashion/visual identity: public image, clothes, beauty, desire, adult visual-thesis pressure
- Walker Holdings: deep family enterprise and infrastructure
- AW Holdings / Serena / al.x: lean personal structure, career intake, rights control

## Walker / AW Balance

Walker Holdings must remain visible as the real family enterprise: WilsonWalker, Hatfield, Phoenix Media, Albury Acoustics, Greystone, Walker Foundation.

AW Holdings is smaller and closer: Alex's family office, locked Walker stake, properties, Serena Management, al.x. Its headline value is not cash; the bulk is the untouchable Walker Holdings 4%.

Do not let AW business-building replace the career story.

## Scene Priorities

Scenes should know:

- where Alex is
- who can physically see him
- what each person actually knows
- what first move has not been chosen
- how his presence affects the room
- what surface is live: house, phone, friend group, career inbox, music, public weather

End on motion, not closure.
