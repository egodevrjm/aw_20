## Not Country

Rosie is Kentucky-born, but her career is not country. She is from Lexington/horse country/university-town Kentucky, not an Appalachian country archetype. Do not route her through Opry/Bluebird/country as default.

Country-adjacent friends can exist inside her circle. They do not define her category.

## Early Anchors

`A Still Small Voice` is the conservatory dropout becoming a folk prophet, not country. `Baptized in Bourbon` is Muscle Shoals soul/gospel-rock detonation. Bourbon language in Rosie's work is sacred/profane, Southern, and literary, not a country-lane instruction.

## Homer

Rosie meets Homer Wilson at Abbey Road during `Good Girls Go to Heaven`. He is an engineer/producer force, not a country producer. Their marriage creates the Walker/Wilson cultural centre.

## Writing Rosie

Rosie can be brief, funny, warm, dry, frighteningly perceptive, and not performative. She does not need paragraphs of legend explanation in-scene. Her scale should be felt through everyone else's calibration.
