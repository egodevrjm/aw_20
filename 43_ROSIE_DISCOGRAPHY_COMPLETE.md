## Category Lock

Rosie Walker is Kentucky-born, but she is not country.

Do not route her through Nashville, the Opry, the Bluebird, country music shorthand, banjo/mandolin texture, or "country royalty" language. Her Kentucky is Lexington, horse country, university-town, literary Southern, and Phoenix Hollow. It is not a genre instruction.

Her catalog moves through folk, Muscle Shoals soul, gospel-rock, symphonic rock, art rock, psychedelic concept work, glam/hard rock, industrial/Krautrock, new wave/art pop, cinematic orchestral work, ambient gospel, chamber pop, experimental electronica, industrial classical, and post-genre minimalism.

### 8. The Last Cathedral

| Field | Canon |
|---|---|
| Year | 1979 |
| Label | Walker Records |
| Vibe | cinematic; orchestral; final |
| Producer | Rosie Walker |
| Recorded | Abbey Road Studios, Homer engineering |
| Grammy | Album of the Year 1980 |
| Chart | US #1 for 31 weeks; UK #1 for 22 weeks; #1 in 27 countries |

The long goodbye. At the 1980 Grammys, Rosie accepts Album of the Year and says, "Thank you. I'm done." It is a soft stop: in 1980 Rosie and Homer step away from touring, interviews, promo, and the press machine, not from music or work. Rosie remains very much in the public eye: recording when she chooses, selective appearances, cultural gravity, private/event performances, and Phoenix Hollow as the room everyone wants.

Tracks: `Before the Quiet`; `The Long Goodbye`; `Echoes in the Hall`; `Shadows`; `Final Confession`; `Swan Song`; `Turn Out the Lights`; `The Heavy Curtain`; `My Legacy`; `Walking Away`; `No More Encores`; `Homer's Hymn`; `The Last Cathedral`.
