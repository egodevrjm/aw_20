## Pricing Rule

Amounts are rough scene-use numbers, not audited contracts. Use them as first-position / initial-package values. Do not recalculate Alex's net worth from them.

Story conversion for quick table use: **GBP 1 = USD 1.25**.

Unless a row explicitly says otherwise, **no figure is monthly**.

Use these bases:

- **Appearance / event fee** = one-off fee for one event, one filming day, one interview, one hosted room, or one performance.
- **Week / festival / race weekend package** = one short defined public window, usually 2-7 days.
- **Project / rights package** = one film, special, release, platform premiere, or limited campaign asset set.
- **Advance / guarantee** = upfront money against rights, recording, distribution, or performance income; not necessarily all cash to Alex personally.
- **Gross** = total event gross before costs, venue, production, tax, splits, management, and charitable allocation.
- **Campaign / ambassador deal** = assumed **year-one / first 12-month global term** unless the row says multi-year. It is not per post, per month, or per appearance.
- **Social activation** = only included if the row says so. Do not assume Alex is posting.

Editorial, public-broadcast, and talk formats often have low direct talent fees. Their value is access, positioning, rights, and downstream leverage. Luxury, music-rights, social, exclusivity, appearances, automotive, tech, and global campaign packages carry the real money.

## Term Rule

For brand, tech, automotive, social-platform, watch/jewellery, fashion, and luxury campaign rows, the listed figure is the **year-one opening ask / first 12-month term** unless the row explicitly says otherwise.

Do not read the table as a full 3-year or 5-year contract value.

When Latham/Serena discusses a longer term, use this language:

- **One-year / 12-month term**: the table figure. This is the opening adult-architecture price.
- **Three-year term**: usually **2.5x-3.5x the year-one number**, depending on exclusivity, renewal options, global usage, social obligations, and category lockout.
- **Five-year term**: usually **4x-6x the year-one number**, with escalators, exit clauses, usage sunsets, and possibly equity, royalties, or foundation commitments.
- **Category exclusivity** raises the number sharply. A phone/tech deal blocking Apple/Samsung/Google/Meta/TikTok; an automotive deal blocking Aston/Bentley/Ford/Tesla; or a fashion deal blocking fragrance/watches/jewellery/menswear can add tens of millions.
- **Usage term matters.** A campaign that can run forever costs much more than a 12-month campaign with a clean sunset.
- **Social obligation matters.** One controlled launch post is not the same as a content calendar. Alex does not sell recurring influencer inventory by default.

Scene shorthand: if Latham says "Apple at 150," he means **roughly £150M for the first year / opening term**, not £150M total over five years and not £150M per month.

- cross-genre musician
- piano principal, but not classical-only
- stylish/funny/social public figure

Wrong frame:

Alex's media test: if he thinks he will enjoy himself and sound like himself, it is viable. If it makes him Serious Prince Pianist, it is probably wrong.
