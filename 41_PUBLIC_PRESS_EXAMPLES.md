# Public Press Examples

Use this for press voice and public archive tone.

## Core Frame

Alex is not newly famous at story open. He is already treated as one of the most photographed and most written-about young private individuals in the world. A normal boring week produces dozens of articles/items globally every day.

The press already has:

- childhood photographs
- royal-adjacent coverage
- Rosie/Homer family context
- Prince Alex headline language
- fashion-week scrutiny
- society genealogy
- polo/country-sport visibility, including his 3-goal handicap in knowledgeable coverage
- music-school coverage
- pre-story-open prestige work: fashion/editorial, brand imagery, public piano performances, broadcast appearances, and family/culture cameos
- paparazzi and party archive
- fan-platform mythology
- heavy China, Korea, Philippines, and Japan coverage equal in heat to Europe
- Asian-market beauty/fashion/fan coverage, amplified by Rosie's huge cultural status there

The 1 August education-first boundary expiry is covered because it changes adult agency, not because it reveals who he is or makes work possible for the first time.

## Press Registers

### Tabloid / Entertainment

Likely angle: the most-watched young man alive has been commercially legible for years and can finally choose adult access, or refuse it.

Avoid: "secret prince revealed."

### Fashion Press

Likely angle: no solo cover or adult profile exists yet; global houses, magazines, platforms, photographers, and stylists want the first adult thesis.

Avoid: inventing a booked cover or campaign.

### Music Press

Likely angle: Electric Ivory was serious, broad, and unreleased; the recording exists but has not been made public.

Avoid: calling him a released recording artist.

### Society / Royal-Adjacent

Likely angle: old public facts, education-first boundary gone, Albury House return, family mythology.

Avoid: acting as if Debrett's/Almanach material was hidden.

### Eligibility / Marriage Watch

Likely angle: the world's most eligible bachelor, never once publicly attached; dynastic-match speculation (European princesses — Princess Leonor of Spain, heir to the Spanish throne, the recurring name) set against celebrity/love-match speculation (models, actresses, musicians, his own legacy-kid friends). "Who will Alex Wilson marry" has run as a story since he turned 18, in both tabloid and society registers.

Avoid: confirming a relationship, inventing a girlfriend, or treating any pairing as real — the speculation is a structure, and no pairing is correct unless Ryan plays it. Full narrative: `06_WORLD_PRESSURES.md`.

### Polo / Country-Sport

Likely angle: Alex as a semi-regular Guards/Cowdray/Cirencester player, 3-goal handicap, position 3/playmaker, in the circuit since Westminster.

Avoid: making polo his job, treating him as a professional player, or writing it as a sudden post-boundary pivot.

### East / Southeast Asian Entertainment And Fashion Press

Likely angle: Rosie's grandson, beautiful royal-adjacent musician, global fashion object, "Prince Alex" mythology, first adult access watch, recycled sightings made huge through edits and translation accounts.

Avoid: treating these markets as secondary, niche, or merely reposting Europe. China, Korea, the Philippines, and Japan are major engines of daily Alex coverage.

### Public Conversation Formats

Likely angle: the first real conversation could happen through many registers: print profile, broadcast interview, podcast, fashion film, music special, late-night couch, global streaming segment, Asian-market entertainment/fashion platform, or a hybrid built around Electric Ivory.

Avoid: assuming British media owns the first access lane, treating comic/broad formats as impossible, or filtering everything through prestige. The question is whether Alex would enjoy himself, sound like himself, and avoid being packaged as Serious Prince Pianist.

### Trade / Business Press

Likely angle: the economics of Alex Wilson — a decade of underpriced access now ending, and what full activation would be worth to the brands, platforms, and media around him. Sober, sourced, analyst-driven (FT, WSJ, Bloomberg, Vogue Business). The 5 August Vogue Business feature is the worked example; see below.

Avoid: tabloid valuation hype, confirming deals, or treating the modelled billion-dollar ecosystem figure as Alex's personal income.

## Albury House Press

Public can say:

- Albury House is Alex's Kensington house.
- It was under scaffolding / dark for more than a year.
- The 7 August Architectural Digest cover story and YouTube video reveal the completed Studio Ashby refurbishment.
- Sophie Ashby takes viewers through five floors — both basement levels, the raised ground floor, and the two guest floors.
- The first-floor Music Nobile (the music room and control room) and the top-floor private suite are not in the feature and stay private.
- The AD feature was shot before Alex's return, with the house empty — it is Sophie Ashby's showcase of her own completed design. Alex does not appear in it at all: no photographs, no footage, no quotes, no presence. NPCs and narration must not treat Alex as visible or featured in the AD piece in any way.
- Published room-by-room interiors and design language for those five floors are public.
- Alex is back in London and in the first post-boundary access watch.

Public cannot say from the AD feature unless Ryan reveals:

- private handover details
- staff/security operations
- hidden systems or safe mechanics
- Serena/AW/al.x details
- unshown private object provenance

## Ashby Reveal

Before 7 August, public/design press could separately say:

- Sophie Ashby / Studio Ashby has been unusually silent for about 18 months.
- Ashby is one of the most in-demand interior designers in society and almost impossible to book.
- Occasional cryptic social posts have fuelled speculation about a major mystery commission.

On 7 August, the mystery resolves publicly as Albury House.

## The 5 August Value Story

On Tuesday 5 August 2025 — two days before the Architectural Digest reveal and the Charles Street meeting — Vogue Business publishes "The Bill Is Due," a senior-analyst feature on the economics of Alex Wilson. The full rendered article is `55_PUBLIC_FOOTPRINT_ARCHIVE.md` Document 6.

This is a dated opening-week trigger. By the time Alex sits down with the Serena/AW inbox on 7 August, the piece is two days old and circulating: the "Alex Wilson economy" framing is already public, and NPCs — friends, family, industry, press — can reference a real article rather than inventing the sentiment.

What the piece establishes publicly:

- For close to a decade, luxury houses accessed the most valuable organic visual asset alive at, effectively, working-model rates — a structural discount the trade now names outright.
- Analysts model a fully active Alex Wilson at north of $1bn a year in *ecosystem value* — earned media, halo-driven sell-through, platform growth, press traffic — and call the figure conservative. This is value to the brands, platforms, and media around him; it is not Alex's earnings.
- Headline component numbers now in public conversation: a first branded TikTok video appraised around $4.5M, a tagged Instagram post around $2.2M, the name/IP itself valued standalone at roughly $350M.

What the piece does not do: confirm any deal, name a chosen first move, or quote Alex or the family. Serena declined to comment. It is a market-modelling exercise, not news of a decision.

Good:

> In Seoul, Tokyo, Manila, and Shanghai, the question is not whether Alex Wilson is famous enough to matter. It is whether the world's most watched beautiful man will finally choose a first adult voice.

Good:

> A Graham Norton sofa would not be the safe route or the unserious route by default. With the right room, it would be a choice about timing, humour, and control: Alex deciding whether the first real conversation can happen in public without becoming a confession.

Good:

> The offer list tells you more about Alex if you stop ranking it by prestige. Newsnight is the wrong room because it makes him a subject. Hot Ones might be the right room because it lets him be funny, physical, musical, ridiculous, and in control.

Bad:

> The world woke up to discover that Alex Wilson was secretly a prince.

Bad:

> Sources confirm Alex has selected his first cover, first label partner, and first campaign.
